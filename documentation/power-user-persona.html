Power SignUp Persona

Name: David Smith

Age: 43

Profession: High School Environmental Science Teacher

Technology: David is a casual technology user, he can do basic tasks but isn't very good at navigating complex websites or applications. He uses an iPhone 11 along with a Windows 11 PC for both work and personal use. He is comfortable using basic technologies like email, web browsers, social media websites, etc.

Frustrations and Needs: David gets frustrated when a website is too complex or not user-friendly, and will abandon the task altogether if it's too difficult. He needs a website that is easy to use and doesn't require a lot of effort to navigate.

Goals: David wants to upload a handful of pictures he took while on a field trip with his class to an area where the ABQ Wildlife Federation did a restoration project.

SignUp Story: I took some great pictures of the landscape while on a field trip and want to upload them to the ABQ Wildlife Federation website to help showcase the impact their work has had over the last few years since they worked on the area.

SignUp Interaction Flow

Use Case: David wants to upload some pictures to the ABQ Wildlife Federation website that he took while on a class field trip so they can be archived and used to demonstrate the positive impact their work has had on the landscape in the area.

Precondition: He has his photos saved to a folder on the desktop of his laptop and is ready to try to upload them.

Postcondition: He was able to successfully upload the photos without any trouble, and can now view them on the ABQ Wildlife Federation website alongside photos from other users.

  • David opens his laptop and launches Google Chrome.
  • Chrome opens the homepage.
  • David clicks on the search bar and types in "ABQ Wildlife Federation" and presses 'Enter'.
  • Chrome loads a page of search results, with the correct website at the very top.
  • David clicks on the first link.
  • The ABQ Wildlife Federation website loads on his screen.
  • David looks over the homepage and sees a navigation bar with a link that says "photos", so he clicks on it.
  • The "photos" link loads a page with a map containing pins with photosets of different worksites around NM.
  • David sees a button at the top that says "upload your own photos here!" and clicks on it.
  • The page loads a new screen.
  • First, David has to make a user account so he enters his information (name, email address, and password) on the page, then clicks on the "create account" button.
  • The page displays a message that says "please check your email to finish setting up your account".
  • David opens a new tab, navigates to his email, and clicks on the new email.
  • The email loads, and he sees a link that says "click here to verify your email address" so he clicks on it.
  • The link opens to the "upload your own photos here!" page again.
  • David now sees a button that says "upload from computer" and clicks on it.
  • The page shows a dropdown menu to select the location where the photos were taken.
  • David selects the correct location from the dropdown menu and clicks "ok".
  • The page loads a selection window with the option to "upload files from your computer".
  • David clicks on the button.
  • The page shows window where you can navigate to the files on your computer and select the ones you want to upload.
  • David navigates to the folder with his field trip photos and selects the ones he wants to upload, then clicks the "ok" button.
  • The photos are uploaded and sorted to the correct location to be stored with other photos from that location.
  • David clicks the navigation bar link that says "photos" again.
  • The page with the map loads again, just like before.
  • David clicks on the map pin in the location his photos were taken.
  • The page loads David's photos alongside photos that have been previously submitted by other users.
